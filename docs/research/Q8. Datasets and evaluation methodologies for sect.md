# Q8. Datasets and evaluation methodologies for section-labeling (intro/verse/chorus/bridge/break/peak/outro) on latin dance music specifically — SALAMI, Harmonix, RWC extensions? Any bachata/kizomba-labeled corpora?

There are currently **no widely used, public section‑labeling datasets that are both Latin‑dance‑specific and cover the full “intro/verse/chorus/bridge/peak/outro” taxonomy**, nor are there curated bachata‑ or kizomba‑labeled corpora with structural labels in the style of SALAMI or Harmonix.[^1_1][^1_2]
However, you can nonetheless build Latin‑dance‑aware section‑labeling methods by leveraging existing structural databases plus Latin‑genre corpora, then adapting or extending their labels.

***

### 1. General section‑labeling datasets you *can* reuse

The main public corpora for intro/verse/chorus/bridge/etc. labels are:

- **SALAMI‑pop** (subset of SALAMI): ~274 songs with hierarchical section boundaries and often functional labels (verse, chorus, bridge, etc.); widely used in MSA work.[^1_3][^1_1]
- **Harmonix Set**: 912 Western pop tracks with beats, downbeats, and sometimes higher‑level structural labels; annotations are primarily for gaming but several MIR studies transcribe them into section types.[^1_4][^1_5]
- **RWC‑Pop**: 100 Japanese‑ and English‑language pop songs with **AIST annotations** including section boundaries (intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro, etc.) and beat/measure labels.[^1_6][^1_7][^1_8]
- MSA benchmarking efforts (e.g., MIREX Music Structure Analysis) now standardize labels to categories such as `intro`, `verse`, `chorus`, `bridge`, `inst`, `outro`, and `silence` across these datasets.[^1_2][^1_1]

These are not Latin‑dance specific, but they do provide a **standard evaluation methodology** for section‑labeling that you can port to Latin data.

***

### 2. Latin‑music and Latin‑dance corpora (section labels scarce)

For Latin‑specific content, the closest things are:

- **Latin Music Database (LMD)**: 3,227 Latin popular and dance recordings (including salsa, merengue, bachata, bolero, etc.), each labeled by “experts in Brazilian dance” with a single genre‑style tag; however, **only pre‑computed features are available, not raw audio or structural labels**.[^1_9][^1_10]
- **Ballroom / Extended Ballroom**: Contains Latin‑derived dance styles (salsa, cha‑cha, rumba, etc.) labeled by dance style and tempo, but again with **no section‑level labels** such as intro/verse/chorus.[^1_11]
- **Salsa‑focused datasets** (e.g., the “Salsa Dataset” and related work) mainly provide beat‑ and rhythm‑level annotations rather than full structural labels.[^1_12][^1_13]

So: **you get Latin‑dance genre labels and beat‑level info, but not section‑labeling labels like SALAMI provides for pop.**

***

### 3. Bachata‑ and kizomba‑specific corpora

For **bachata** and **kizomba**:

- There are **no public, peer‑reviewed, section‑labeled corpora** for bachata or kizomba with intro/verse/chorus/bridge/peak/outro labels.[^1_14][^1_9]
- Bachata is sometimes discussed in terms of **polyrhythmic sections** (derecho in verses, majao in choruses, mambo in bridges/solos), but those descriptions are pedagogical/musicological, not tied to an annotated dataset.[^1_14]
- Kizomba appears in streaming‑analytics and metadata corpora (e.g., Spotify‑based snapshots), again only for genre or popularity metrics, not structural labels.[^1_15]

In other words, **you would have to build your own bachata/kizomba section‑labeling corpus** if you want SALAMI‑style annotations for those genres.

***

### 4. Practical strategy for your use case

If you want to do **section‑labeling on Latin dance music**, a reasonable pipeline is:

1. **Use SALAMI / Harmonix / RWC‑Pop** as a **template methodology** (boundary detection + label taxonomy) and report metrics directly comparable to MSA literature.[^1_1][^1_2]
2. **Collect Latin‑dance tracks** (Salsa, bachata, kizomba, etc.) from LMD‑style or ballroom‑style corpora, then:
    - Manually annotate a subset with SALAMI‑style labels (intro/verse/chorus/bridge/inst/peak/outro/other).[^1_2][^1_1]
    - Consider using **dance‑style or beat‑annotations** (from salsa/ballroom datasets) as auxiliary features or constraints.[^1_12][^1_11]
3. **Repurpose or extend** existing MSA datasets (e.g., hook‑ or MuSFA‑style functional‑label sets) to your Latin‑dance setting, mapping e.g., “intro, verse, chorus, bridge, inst, outro, silence” as in the MIREX MSA task.[^1_1][^1_2]
