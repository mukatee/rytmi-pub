# Q10. Prior work combining DSP-derived structure with LLM-generated music explanation / coaching — any published systems that use structured audio analysis as grounding for natural-language music education?

Yes—there is now a small but growing body of published work that explicitly combines DSP‑ or signal‑derived structure with LLMs to generate natural‑language music explanations, coaching, or tutoring. However, most of these systems are still in the research or prototype stage and are not yet widely known “products” in mainstream music‑ed circles.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_3]

### Key published systems

1. **MuseAgent‑1 (MuseAgent‑1: Interactive Grounded Multimodal Understanding of Music)**[^1_2]
    - Combines an LLM with automatic music transcription (AMT) and optical‑music‑recognition front‑ends to ground reasoning in both symbolic scores and performance audio.
    - Uses the extracted, structured musical representation (measures, notes, timing, dynamics) to power interactive music‑theory and performance‑evaluation tasks, including natural‑language feedback and coaching‑style explanations.[^1_2]
2. **DeepResonance (multimodal music‑understanding LLM)**[^1_4][^1_1]
    - A music‑focused LLM fine‑tuned on music‑centric datasets that include audio, text, images, and video.
    - While not a “coaching” system per se, it is designed to interpret musical content through multiple aligned modalities and can generate explanations that are grounded in both audio and metadata/visual features, making it a candidate backbone for music‑instruction interfaces.[^1_1][^1_4]
3. **Pitch‑feature‑matching music tutoring system**[^1_3]
    - A signal‑processing‑based music‑tutoring system that extracts pitch features from MIDI or score files and uses feature matching to recommend practice targets and learning paths.
    - The tutoring logic is rule‑based rather than fully LLM‑driven, but the core idea—grounding pedagogical advice in structured audio/score features—is closely aligned with the setup you describe.[^1_3]
4. **NIME 2022 “computer‑aided multimodal music learning system with curriculum”**[^1_5]
    - Describes an AI‑empowered music‑tutor interface that uses multimodal inputs (audio, gesture, etc.) and a curriculum‑aware backend to guide students.
    - The system is more GUI‑ and interaction‑oriented, but the signal‑processing front‑end provides structured feedback that could be paired with LLM‑generated explanations in later extensions.[^1_5]

### Where this fits your “Q10” phrasing

In terms of your question—“prior work combining DSP‑derived structure with LLM‑generated music explanation / coaching”—the clearest *direct* matches are:

- **MuseAgent‑1**, which explicitly grounds LLM‑style reasoning over music‑theory and performance‑analysis tasks in DSP‑style and AMT‑derived structure.[^1_2]
- The **pitch‑feature‑matching tutoring system**, which uses signal‑derived features (pitch contours, etc.) to generate pedagogical suggestions, even if those are currently rule‑based rather than LLM‑driven.[^1_3]

For a literature‑review or methods‑section citation, you could frame these as:

- “MuseAgent‑1 integrates an LLM with automatic music transcription and score‑audio alignment to support interactive music‑theory reasoning and performance‑evaluation explanations.”[^1_2]
- “Prior DSP‑based tutoring systems have used pitch‑feature matching from MIDI or score files to generate personalized learning suggestions, demonstrating that structured audio analysis can ground music‑instruction logic.”[^1_3]

If you say a bit more about the *genre* (e.g., classical, pop, jazz) or *output type* (e.g., beginner‑friendly metaphors vs. formal theory), it’s possible to narrow to a tighter list of cite‑worthy examples.
