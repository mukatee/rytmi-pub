# Q9. Unsupervised vs. supervised structure-segmentation baselines (MSAF, SegBaseline, neural boundary detection) — reported F-measures at 0.5 s / 3 s tolerance on popular music, and failure modes on repetitive arrangements.

For unsupervised vs. supervised structure‑segmentation baselines on *popular music*, the typical boundary‑detection F‑measures at 0.5 s and 3 s tolerance are roughly in the mid‑0.4 to low‑0.6 F‑measure range, with supervised neural boundary detectors generally outperforming traditional unsupervised methods such as MSAF’s spectral‑clustering and SegBaseline‑style checkerboard‑kernel approaches on standard corpora like SALAMI, Beatles‑TUT, or RWC‑Popular.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_3][^1_4]

### Typical F‑measure ranges

- **Unsupervised baselines (MSAF, SegBaseline‑style SSM + Foote‑kernel):**
    - Popular music datasets often report F‑measures around **0.40–0.50 at 0.5 s tolerance** and **0.45–0.55 at 3 s tolerance**, depending on dataset and feature choices (e.g., PCP vs. MFCC, beat‑synchronous vs. frame‑wise).[^1_2][^1_4][^1_1]
- **Supervised neural boundary detectors (CNN, LSTM, or hybrid models):**
    - Recent supervised boundary nets on similar data report F‑measures around **0.50–0.65 at 0.5 s tolerance** and sometimes **0.55–0.65 at 3 s**, outperforming the above unsupervised baselines on the same tracks.[^1_3][^1_4][^1_5]

(MSAF’s own evaluation interface reports the same “HitRate_0.5F” and “HitRate_3F” definitions used in these works, so the numbers are directly comparable.)[^1_6][^1_7][^1_8]

### Failure modes on repetitive arrangements

On heavily repetitive arrangements (verse–chorus loops, static loops, EDM‑style repetition), both classes of model struggle, but the failure modes differ:

- **Unsupervised / SSM‑based methods (MSAF, SegBaseline):**
    - Tend to **over‑segment** inside repeated sections because local novelty or checkerboard‑kernel peaks misinterpret small variations (e.g., drum fills, slight timbral changes) as meaningful boundaries.[^1_4][^1_1]
    - Conversely, they can **under‑segment** when transitions between sections are very gradual or when the repetition pattern dominates the self‑similarity matrix, causing the model to ignore subtle structural changes.[^1_1][^1_4]
- **Neural boundary detectors (supervised CNNs, RNNs):**
    - Often **over‑fit melodic or rhythmic motifs** and fail to generalize across different arrangements, producing inconsistent boundaries when the same motif appears in slightly different contexts.[^1_5][^1_3]
    - May also exhibit **“boundary‑clustering”** around strong onsets or drum hits instead of higher‑level structural transitions, especially if training data is biased toward explicit, onset‑driven boundaries.[^1_9][^1_3]

In repetitive arrangements, both unsupervised and supervised systems benefit from **explicit segment‑duration modeling** and **multi‑level self‑similarity / context‑aware features**, which help distinguish between local repetitions and global section changes.[^1_4][^1_5][^1_1]
