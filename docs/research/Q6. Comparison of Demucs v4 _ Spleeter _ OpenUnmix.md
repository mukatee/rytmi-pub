# Q6. Comparison of Demucs v4 / Spleeter / OpenUnmix / HTDemucs as vocal-stem sources for downstream vocal-activity classification — robustness on latin styles (where vocals are breathier / mid-heavy) and CPU/GPU cost.

For downstream vocal‑activity classification on Latin‑style tracks (breathy, mid‑heavy vocals), **HTDemucs / Demucs v4 generally give the cleanest, most robust vocal stems**, at the cost of higher CPU/GPU load; **Spleeter and OpenUnmix trade separation quality for lower compute** and are often “good enough” for activity‑based classifiers if you can tolerate some leakage.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_3][^1_4]

Below is a practical comparison for your use case.

***

### 1. Vocal‑separation quality on Latin‑style vocals

| Model | Vocal‑separation quality (mid‑heavy / breathy) | Notes relevant to Latin styles |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **HTDemucs / Demucs v4** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (best) [^1_1][^1_3][^1_5] | Hybrid waveform‑spectrogram model with transformer‑based context; achieves highest SDR (~9 dB on MUSDB‑HQ) and cleanly isolates vocals even in dense, mid‑heavy mixes. [^1_1][^1_3][^1_5] |
| **Demucs v4 (non‑HT)** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Very clean, but slightly behind HTDemucs on hard cases; still better than Spleeter on breathy vocals. [^1_1][^1_5] |
| **Spleeter** | ⭐⭐⭐ | Uses spectrogram masking; can leave “watery” vocal artifacts and more instrumental bleed, especially in mid‑range‑heavy Latin arrangements. [^1_1][^1_2][^1_6] |
| **OpenUnmix** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (vocals only) [^1_3][^1_4] | Designed for instrument‑centric separation, but can do decent vocal isolation if you care more about magnitude than waveform fidelity; mid‑range vocal preservation is reasonable but not as clean as HTDemucs on complex mixes. [^1_3][^1_4] |

For **robustness on breathy, mid‑heavy Latin vocals**, a vocal‑only HTDemucs‑style stem or a **Demucs‑derived vocal stem** will typically give the most reliable activation signal for a downstream VAD / classifier, because of reduced leakage and phase artifacts.[^1_3][^1_7][^1_1]

***

### 2. CPU / GPU cost and latency

| Model | Typical 3–4 min track (CPU) | Typical 3–4 min track (GPU) | Memory / HW footprint |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Spleeter** | 15–30 s on modern CPU [^1_2][^1_8] | 3–5 s on GPU [^1_2] | ~150 MB model, ~2 GB RAM; runs comfortably on modest laptops. [^1_2][^1_9] |
| **OpenUnmix** | Very fast, similar or faster than Spleeter [^1_4] | Fast; sub‑real‑time on GPU [^1_4] | Relatively small, designed for real‑time‑style use. [^1_4] |
| **Demucs v4** | ~5–15 min on CPU, depending on CPU [^1_8][^1_10] | ~20–45 s on consumer GPU (RTX‑class). [^1_2][^1_8] | ~2 GB model, 6–8 GB RAM typical; transformer‑based so needs beefier system or GPU. [^1_2][^1_8][^1_5] |
| **HTDemucs** | ~8–25 min on CPU [^1_8][^1_10] | ~30–90 s on GPU; fastest on RTX‑4090‑class. [^1_2][^1_8] | Highest VRAM/RAM demand of the four; transformer‑heavy and attention‑bound. [^1_3][^1_5] |

For **downstream vocal‑activity classification**, if you care about **inference‑time speed** and are running on CPU‑only or low‑end hardware, **Spleeter or OpenUnmix** are the most practical; if you care about **accuracy and robustness** on Latin‑style vocals and can afford GPU, **HTDemucs or Demucs v4** are strongly preferable.[^1_2][^1_8][^1_1][^1_3]

***

### 3. Practical recommendation for your pipeline

- **Best robustness on Latin‑style vocals:**
Use **HTDemucs vocal‑only** or **Demucs v4 vocal stem** as the VAD source, then feed the clean vocal‑only waveform into a standard VAD or simple energy‑based classifier. This minimizes leakage from guitars, horns, and percussive mid‑range that can confuse binary vocal‑activity decisions.[^1_7][^1_11][^1_1][^1_3]
- **If you must run on CPU / low‑end GPU:**
Use **Spleeter 2‑stems (vocals vs accompaniment)** or **OpenUnmix**; they are still adequate for high‑level vocal‑activity detection when the vocal is clearly present, though you may need stronger post‑filtering (e.g., thresholding, VAD‑driven gating) to handle leakage.[^1_4][^1_12][^1_2]
