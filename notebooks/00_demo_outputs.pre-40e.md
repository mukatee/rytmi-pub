Kizomba typically unfolds within a comfortable tempo range of 85–110 BPM in a 4/4 time signature. Rather than relying on a heavy percussive grid, the rhythmic pulse is often carried by a deep, driving bass line that invites a fluid connection to the music. Angolan kizomba often features a more traditional, grounded pulse, while urbankiz tends to have a more polished, electronic texture and higher energy. Bachata is a rhythmic dance set in 4/4 time, typically played within a tempo range of 120–135 BPM. The pulse is traditionally driven by a combination of melodic guitar patterns and percussive elements like bongos, which provide a clear rhythmic grid. While the underlying beat remains steady, the music often features syncopation that defines its unique feel.
