Kizomba typically operates within a comfortable tempo range of 85–110 BPM in a 4/4 time signature. Rather than relying on a heavy drum kit, the rhythmic pulse is often carried by a deep, driving bass line and melodic textures, making the rhythm feel felt rather than heard. While the underlying pulse remains steady, you will often encounter syncopation and emphasis on off-beats that create a flowing, circular motion. Angolan kizomba often features a more traditional, grounded pulse, while urbankiz tends to have a more polished, electronic feel with higher energy.
